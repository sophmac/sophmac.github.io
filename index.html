About me (with lots of links)

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the University of British Columbia (UBC), under the supervision of Brian Marcus, Lior Silberman, and Omer Angel. My research is in an area called symbolic dynamics, which involves concepts and techniques from dynamical systems and theoretical computer science.

I finished my Master's (MSc) in Mathematics in August 2019, also in the UBC Mathematics Department, under the supervision of Gordon Slade. My Master's essay was a survey of a few results about entropy and computability in lattice systems, like the Ising model. I hope to produce a human-readable version of this document at some point. Before that, I finished my Hons Bsc in Mathematics and Statistics at McMaster University in April 2017. My undergraduate research topics were on electrochemistry (supervised by Bartek Protas), Brownian motion (supervised by Walter Craig, now sadly deceased), and random matrix theory (supervised by Masoud Khalkhali through the Fields Institute's Undergraduate Summer Research Program).

Apart from research, I have been a teaching assistant in the Mathematics Department for a few years, although I'm currently a full-time research assistant. I also facilitate workshops on teaching and presentation skills through UBC's Centre for Teaching, Learning, and Technology (CTLT), and have worked as a tutor for the Indigenous Teacher Education Program (NITEP).
